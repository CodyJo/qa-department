🔎 QA Department
How is the Health Score calculated?

The Health Score starts at 100 and deducts points for each open finding based on severity:

score = 100 - (critical × 15) - (high × 8) - (medium × 3) - (low × 1) floor = 0 (score never goes below zero)

Info-level findings don't affect the score. Only open findings count — fixed items are excluded.

What do the severity levels mean?
SeverityPenaltyDefinition
Critical-15Security vulnerabilities or complete functional failures that block users
High-8Significant bugs or security issues that degrade the experience
Medium-3Moderate issues affecting performance, reliability, or code quality
Low-1Minor issues, code style, or improvement opportunities
Info0Documentation and best practice recommendations
What categories does QA scan?

The QA agent scans for: security vulnerabilities, performance bottlenecks, reliability bugs, code-quality issues, and testing gaps. It also runs the project's linter, test suite, and type checker when available.

📈 SEO & AI Optimization
How is the SEO Score calculated?

The SEO Score is a weighted average of 5 category scores:

score = (technical × 0.30) + (ai × 0.20) + (content × 0.20) + (performance × 0.20) + (social × 0.10)

Each category starts at 100 and deducts: critical × 15, high × 8, medium × 4, low × 2. Info findings are free.

What are the 5 SEO categories?
CategoryWeightCovers
Technical SEO30%Crawlability, indexing, sitemaps, robots.txt, canonical URLs, structured data
AI Optimization20%LLM discoverability, llms.txt, schema markup for AI, content structure for AI citation
Content SEO20%Meta titles/descriptions, heading hierarchy, alt text, internal linking, keyword usage
Performance SEO20%Core Web Vitals signals, image optimization, lazy loading, render-blocking resources
Social Meta10%Open Graph tags, Twitter cards, preview images, social sharing readiness
♿ ADA Compliance
How is the Compliance Score calculated?

Same deduction formula as QA:

score = 100 - (critical × 15) - (high × 8) - (medium × 3) - (low × 1)

Only open findings count. Info-level (AAA aspirational) items have zero penalty.

How is the WCAG Level determined?

The WCAG conformance level is determined by which levels have open issues:

  • AAA — Score ≥ 95 and zero critical, high, or medium findings
  • AA — Score ≥ 70 and zero critical findings
  • A — Score ≥ 40 and zero critical findings blocking Level A criteria
  • Non-Compliant — Score < 40 or critical findings on Level A criteria

Each finding is tagged with the WCAG criterion it violates (e.g., 1.1.1, 2.4.7) and the level (A, AA, or AAA) that criterion belongs to.

What are the POUR categories?

WCAG 2.1 organizes accessibility into four principles, scored independently:

PrincipleCovers
PerceivableAlt text, captions, contrast, text resizing, non-text contrast
OperableKeyboard access, no traps, timing, skip navigation, focus visible
UnderstandableLanguage tags, predictable behavior, form labels, error handling
RobustValid HTML, ARIA roles/states, screen reader compatibility
🛡️ Regulatory Compliance
How is the overall Compliance Score calculated?

The overall score is the average of three framework scores:

score = (GDPR + ISO27001 + AgeVerification) / 3

Each framework is scored independently with its own deduction formula:

framework = 100 - (critical × 25) - (high × 15) - (medium × 8) - (low × 3) - (info × 1)

Note: Regulatory compliance uses heavier penalties than other departments because violations carry legal risk.

What do the framework status levels mean?
  • Compliant — Score ≥ 90
  • Partial — Score 60–89
  • Non-Compliant — Score < 60
What frameworks are audited?
FrameworkCovers
GDPRCookie consent, privacy policy, data subject rights, data processing agreements, breach notification
ISO 27001Information security policies, access control, encryption, logging, incident response, secure development
Age VerificationUS state laws (COPPA, Utah, Texas, etc.) and UK Online Safety Act age verification requirements
💰 Monetization Strategy
How is the Monetization Readiness Score calculated?

The Readiness Score is a weighted average of 4 dimensions, each scored 0–100:

score = (infrastructure × 0.25) + (audience × 0.25) + (features × 0.25) + (market_fit × 0.25)
DimensionWeightMeasures
Infrastructure25%Payment processing, user accounts, content management, API maturity
Audience25%Traffic volume, engagement metrics, purchasing power signals
Features25%How close existing features are to being monetizable
Market Fit25%Competitive pricing benchmarks, niche demand, timing
What do the opportunity value levels mean?
  • High Value — $500+/month potential with strong product-market fit
  • Medium Value — $100–500/month potential or high potential with significant implementation cost
  • Low Value — $10–100/month or speculative/experimental opportunity
What revenue categories are evaluated?

Seven categories: Display Advertising, Affiliate Marketing, Premium Features, Print Fulfillment, Digital Products, Client Services, and Sponsorships. Each is evaluated for feasibility, effort, and revenue potential specific to the target site.

🗺️ Product Roadmap
How is the Product Readiness Score calculated?

The Readiness Score is a weighted average of 4 dimensions:

score = (features × 0.30) + (ux × 0.25) + (technical × 0.25) + (growth × 0.20)
DimensionWeightScoring Method
Feature Completeness30%Starts at 100, deducts by severity of feature-gap findings
UX Polish25%Starts at 100, deducts by severity of UX-improvement findings
Technical Health25%Starts at 100, deducts by severity of tech-debt findings
Growth Potential20%Starts at 50, adds points for identified opportunities (capped at 100)

Note: Growth Potential is unique — it starts neutral at 50 and increases as more growth opportunities are identified. The more opportunities found, the higher the score.

How are items prioritized?
  • Must-Have — Blocking user value or causing churn
  • Should-Have — Noticeably improves the product
  • Nice-to-Have — Polishes the experience
  • Future — Long-term roadmap items
  • Idea — Exploration and brainstorm
⚙️ General
How is the "Last Scanned" time determined?

The timestamp is stamped by the system when the agent finishes writing its findings file — not by the agent itself. This ensures the time is always accurate UTC regardless of what the AI model reports.

How often do dashboards refresh?

Department dashboards auto-refresh data every 60 seconds. The Jobs dashboard refreshes every 3 seconds while scans are running, slowing to 60 seconds when idle.
